Risk Management Matrix

We Solved The Hard Problems

Implementing drone logistics in mountainous terrain involves regulatory, environmental, and safety challenges. Here is our detailed mitigation strategy.

Regulatory Compliance (BVLOS)

Problem: Taiwan CAA has strict "Beyond Visual Line of Sight" rules.

Our Solution: The Regulatory Sandbox

We are not flying illegally. We have partnered with ThunderTiger Corp to apply for the Unmanned Vehicle Technology Innovation Experimentation Act.

  • All flights operate in pre-approved corridors away from population centers.
  • Pilots hold valid G2 licenses (Professional Rating).
  • Operations are conducted under the supervision of the Taichung Transportation Bureau.

Weather & Terrain Reliability

Problem: Heping District has strong mountain winds and sudden rain.

Our Solution: Hybrid Protocol

We don't force flights in bad weather. We use a Hybrid Model:

  • Good Weather: Drones deliver hot meals (15 mins).
  • Bad Weather (>10m/s wind): System alerts "Ground Force". Local volunteer motorcyclists take over.
  • Disaster Prep: Drones pre-stock 3 days of dried food at elders' homes before typhoon seasons.

Elderly Safety & Interaction

Problem: Spinning propellers are dangerous for frail seniors.

Our Solution: The Winch-Drop System

Our drones never land at the beneficiary's home.

1. Drone hovers at 15 meters altitude (safe distance).
2. Package is lowered via a tether winch.
3. Package auto-detaches upon touching the ground.

This ensures zero physical contact between the elder and the drone mechanics.
